Review: I Have No Mouth And I Must Scream

In which we discuss the misadventures of a rogue AI that has annhilated most of humanity, and tortures the few that are left.

There's a lot that could be said about this book. It was published in 1967, yet the terrible world within its pages is just as relavent to the fears of people today. I heard about this book in an Elon Musk interview. Him, along with Bill Gates, Steven Hawking, and a lot of other really smart guys are united in their fear of the possibility of a rogue AI. They aren't alone either. Movies, books, and all other forms of media proliferate this concept. Ask anyone, it's probably a concept they've thought about or heard about at one point or another.

  • 2001: A Space Odysset
  • Ex Machina
  • Portal
  • Transcendence

Both creative and smart people have been trying to warn us for decades about the dangers of AI.
